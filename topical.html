Starlink

Starlink is a satellite constellation in development by SpaceX. In 2022 Starlink consisted of 2300 small satellites.

Overcoming the limitations of cabels

Today the global infrastructure of the internet is, to a large degree, dependent on physical sub-sea and underground cables. Despite the global nature of the internet, the very fact that it relies on physical cables makes its flow of information sometimes subject to national borders. One recent example of this is the new Norwegian E-Law,[10] which propose that the intelligence service can monitor and collect all data that goes across the national border. As society becomes increasingly digitalized, internet cables can also be seen as critical infrastructure. Internet cables, both in the North-Sea and of the coast of Taiwan is suspected to have been deliberately sabotaged. An estimated 2,5 billion people in the world do not yet have access to the internet.[11] To overcome the physical limitations of internet cables, companies like Starlink and the EU are working on providing internet access through satellites. Elon Musks Starlink is today the largest global provider of satellite internet with over 4,500 satellites in orbit. They expect to have 42,000 new ones in orbit in the coming years.[12]

Creating opportunities

Connectivity to areas of the world with no internet gives a lot of new opportunities, such as access to educational resources, and develop academic skills thats needed to get work opportunities.[2] Security and availability. Especially when natural or non-natural disasters occurs. This was for example demonstrated during the Russian invasion of Ukraine, where Starlink ensured Ukraine free internet.[1] Infrastructures have a positive effect on economic growth and provide the opportunity to develop the economy by providing jobs and promote the exchange of goods and services.[5]

Expensive

A negative aspect of satellite internet is that it is relatively expensive compared to broadband internet, which will make it less accessible [3]. Satellite internet technology can also have delay times and slower speeds compared to broadband internet. This is because of the distance between the satellite and your satellite dish. The people who will notice this the most are those who play video games [3]. There’s also the question if this infrastructure is sustainable long term because of collisions with space debris and new satellites. It’s estimated by 2028 that Starlink satellites will have to manoeuvre a million times per half-year to reduce the risk of orbital collisions.[6]

In theory this will make society better. As the exchange of information and knowledge is more available for everyone. Rural and undeveloped areas will get all the benefits and opportunities the internet can give. However, we also believe there will be challenges with the use of this new infrastructure. In 1.3, Hanne mentions how infrastructure can change and affect out physical environment. With Starlink the management of our orbital space will be questioned as more and more satellites increase the risk for orbital collisions. Yet SpaceX is not the only player. “Other players all over the world, including Amazon with its project Kuiper and China with Guowang, are scrambling to secure orbital slots with their respective regulators”.[6] Example of non-physical changes to rural and undeveloped areas are the access to educational and economic resources, new work, and therefore hopefully economic growth. However, in this case it’s also dependent on the vision and values of SpaceX. What’s the intended purpose with Starlink? Are these intentions transparent to the public? Given the cost of launching these satellites, will poor people who might benefit the most, get access to this infrastructure?

Radiation

An example of an unintended consequence is radiation from the satellites that can accumulate and interfere with other kinds of radio telescopes. Since Starlink is set to grow to 12000 satellites, from the now 4000, there is bound to be more interference. However, Starlink is now working with scientist to identify and isolate the issues.[9] Some people may lose their jobs like cable and internet services because of the new technology. This will also make people less active. Radioactive waves can be harmful to our health and ultimately nature can be damaged. Satellite internet can lead to less disturbance in the environment considering that there is no need for large cables or wires to be laid on the ground. However, satellite internet can still cause negative consequences. The satellites that are placed in orbit around the world can contribute to space debris. The satellites that have a malfunction or reach the end of their life remain in orbit as debris or they can damage other satellites or spacecraft.[4] With satellite internet, people will more easily get in touch with family and friends around the world in rural or undeveloped areas. It can lead to a more globalized culture with new ideas and perspectives from other countries. It connects people, businesses and organizations worldwide, which can lead to cultural exchange and collaboration. There may also be economic consequences since the satellite are more expensive than the traditional cabled internet.[3] Therefore, private companies may want to take a higher fee for using their platform, and then the availability will be limited again only to the wealthier of the population. Because of this the people that will benefit from this technology might be limited to the wealthy or corporations that use it for work related matters.
